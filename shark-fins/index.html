What Are Shark Fins?

Sharks have several rigid fins along their bodies. Unlike the soft, flexible fins of many bony fish, shark fins are stiffened by ceratotrichia — thin, elastic fibers made of protein — which keep the fin shape steady while the shark swims. Each fin has a specific job: some keep the shark from rolling over, some create lift, some steer, and one powers the shark forward. Together, these fins make sharks some of the most efficient swimmers in the ocean.

The 5 Main Types of Fins

Click through the cards below — the highlighted (orange) fin on each diagram shows where that fin sits on the shark's body.

Dorsal Fin

The tall fin on the shark's back — the one you often see slicing above the water. It acts like a keel, keeping the shark stable and preventing it from rolling side to side while it swims.

Pectoral Fins

A pair of fins near the head, one on each side. They work like airplane wings, creating lift so the shark doesn't sink, and they help it steer up, down, and side to side.

Pelvic Fins

A smaller pair of fins on the underside, further back than the pectoral fins. They add extra stability and balance, and in male sharks are modified into claspers used for reproduction.

Anal Fin

A single small fin on the underside, just in front of the tail (not all sharks have one). Like the dorsal fin, it helps stop the shark from rolling and keeps it swimming in a straight line.

Caudal Fin (Tail)

The tail fin is the shark's engine — powerful side-to-side sweeps push the shark forward. Most sharks have a "heterocercal" tail, meaning the top lobe is longer than the bottom lobe.

Fun Facts About Shark Fins

  • 🦴Shark fins are stiffened by fibers called ceratotrichia, not bone — sharks have a skeleton made of cartilage, not bone.
  • 📈The dorsal fin is often the only part of a shark visible above the water's surface.
  • 🌀A shark's tail is called "heterocercal," meaning the upper lobe is longer than the lower lobe — unlike the symmetrical tails of most bony fish.
  • ✈️Pectoral fins work like airplane wings, using water flow to generate lift and keep a shark from sinking.
  • ♂️In male sharks, the pelvic fins include claspers, specialized structures used during reproduction.
  • 🐟Not every shark species has an anal fin — its presence or absence helps scientists classify different shark species.
